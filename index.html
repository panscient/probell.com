About

I began my career as a chip designer, eventually managing the patent portfolio for an AI software company. I have worked for seven startups (two went public), which was enough to become an investor.

Along the way I paused to study the history of humanity's compounding development of technologies: language, agriculture, metals, mechanical devices, carbon fuels, electricity, radio, space. I have lived through four of the consequential waves.

Wikipedia is my favorite website. I enjoy writing and teaching because that's the best way to learn. I enjoy cycling and tend a fine garden. I watch live theater but not television. I like cloudy weather and wind.

I love to travel. I have spent time in most US states and many nations, and have a friend in each one. Though I wander, I spend most of my time in Silicon Valley, the world's largest magnet for innovators.

I enjoy trying new languages but know only one fluently. Lately: Mandarin and traditional Chinese characters. I enjoy limericks, wordplay, thoughtful sayings, and general trivia. I view strangers as friends I have yet to meet. I like challenging dogma with reason. I will take either side for the sake of a good debate.
